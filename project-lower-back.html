Lower Back Muscle Force Estimation

Overview

Developed a musculoskeletal modeling approach to estimate lower back muscle forces during lifting tasks. Used OpenSim and wearable IMUs for real-time biomechanical analysis.

Problem Statement

Lower back injuries are common in manual labor, but accurate force estimation is difficult due to limited field-measurement methods. Traditional approaches rely on inverse dynamics models, which can be computationally complex.

Solution

  • Used OpenSim to model lumbar spine loading based on kinematic data.
  • Integrated IMU-based motion tracking for real-time data collection.
  • Validated results against motion capture systems for accuracy.

Impact & Future Scope

  • Enhanced injury prevention strategies through precise force estimation.
  • Potential integration with wearable exoskeletons for real-time monitoring.
  • Future applications in workplace injury prevention & ergonomic assessments.
