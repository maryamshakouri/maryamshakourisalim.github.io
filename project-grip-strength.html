Grip Strength Biofeedback System

Overview

Developed a biofeedback system integrating a grip force sensor and an Android app to assist individuals with hand rehabilitation after injury or spinal cord injury. The system provides real-time grip strength feedback and tracks progress through an interactive digital interface.

Problem Statement

Rehabilitation for hand injuries often involves grip-strengthening exercises prescribed by clinicians. However, patients may lose motivation due to a lack of real-time feedback and progress tracking. Additionally, clinicians lack quantifiable data to monitor improvements remotely.

Solution

  • Designed a pressure-sensitive circuit to measure grip strength variations in real time.
  • Implemented a wireless data transfer system using Bluetooth to send grip strength readings to a mobile app.
  • Developed a user-friendly Android application that visualizes grip strength in real time through a game-based interface.
  • Stored grip strength data to allow clinicians to monitor improvement trends remotely.

Impact & Future Scope

  • Increased therapy adherence through interactive feedback and gamification.
  • Quantifiable rehabilitation progress for clinicians to track patient recovery more effectively.
  • Future expansion to wearable sensors for more comprehensive rehabilitation monitoring.
