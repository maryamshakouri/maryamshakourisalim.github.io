Assessing Passive Exoskeletons for Industrial Work

Overview

Evaluated passive exoskeletons for reducing ergonomic risks in manual lifting tasks. Analyzed muscle activation, posture changes, and comfort in industrial settings.

Problem Statement

Industrial workers performing repetitive lifting are prone to muscle fatigue and injuries. While passive exoskeletons offload strain, their effectiveness in real work environments is not well studied.

Solution

  • Measured muscle activation patterns using wearable EMG sensors.
  • Tracked postural changes & task efficiency with motion capture data.
  • Conducted user feedback assessments on comfort & usability.

Impact & Future Scope

  • Provided data-driven insights into passive exoskeleton effectiveness.
  • Helped refine exoskeleton designs for better workplace integration.
  • Future work could explore AI-driven motion adaptation for assistive wearables.
